There are two types of "directness":

1. Direct Response (which maximises profits)

2. Direct Copy (which stops you looking salesy)

 

I'll make you more money

while protecting your brand and reputation

with these two elements.

1. Use Direct Response to Maximise Profits

"direct response marketing" = any piece of marketing that demands a direct response from the customer.

That response can be anything that moves them through the customer funnel, from using a cut-out coupon, to signing up to an email newsletter, to pulling out their card and buying from you.

And now it gets interesting… 

The power of these techniques is in how easily you can measure results. It gets quite scientific. If something isn't working, you can tweak it slightly, test it again, repeat, and discover exactly what's working, and what's not.

Which keeps practitioners like myself honest.

If something isn't working, there's no way to hide from it. It's spelled out in a graph of sales data!

2. Use Direct Copy to Earn Trust and Respect

"direct copywriting" is my own term - it's what I call writing that feels straightforward and trustworthy.

I resisted calling myself a "marketer" for a long time.

Like any form of power, marketing skills can be misused, which is why we were all brought up with a mistrust of marketers.

But eventually I learned enough about how money and attention moves in the world to realise that it is mostly down to marketing.

No use crying about it, or wishing that the quality of your product can be enough to have people stream through your doors.

It's the nature of the game.

And you can play it differently.

The fact that people often mistrust sales copy (and salespeople) gives you an advantage. It means the bar is low, and it's easy for you to be refreshing and compelling to your customers. Direct copy takes less effort than sneaky copy, and it's more effective.

In fact, the more trustworthy your words feel to people, the less you'll have to try.
